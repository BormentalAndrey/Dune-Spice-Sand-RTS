## 📚 Lore Accuracy Statement

This game is built with **absolute fidelity** to Frank Herbert's original six Dune novels:

- *Dune* (1965)
- *Dune Messiah* (1969)
- *Children of Dune* (1976)
- *God Emperor of Dune* (1981)
- *Heretics of Dune* (1984)
- *Chapterhouse: Dune* (1985)

**Core Fidelity Rules:**
- ❌ **NO** artificial intelligence or thinking machines (Butlerian Jihad)
- ❌ **NO** movie-only elements from Lynch or Villeneuve adaptations
- ✅ **ONLY** elements from Brian Herbert & Kevin J. Anderson that do NOT contradict Frank's vision
- ✅ Every mechanic cites specific book chapters
- ✅ Fremen culture, Bene Gesserit teachings, and Imperial politics are book-accurate

**"The spice must flow."** — *Dune, Book I, Chapter 1*

## 🎮 Game Overview

**Dune: Spice & Sand** is a real-time strategy game set on the desert planet Arrakis. Players command one of three factions—House Atreides, House Harkonnen, or the Fremen—in the struggle for control of the spice melange, the most valuable substance in the universe.

### Story
The game follows the events of Frank Herbert's *Dune* through 18 campaign missions, from Duke Leto Atreides' arrival on Arrakis to Paul Muad'Dib's transformation and the jihad that reshapes the universe.

### Gameplay
- Harvest spice while avoiding sandworms
- Build bases with book-accurate structures
- Command units with unique abilities
- Use prescience, the Voice, and other powers
- Navigate political intrigue and alliances

## ✨ Features

### Core Mechanics
- **Spice Economy** – The only strategic resource. Melange extends life, grants prescience, powers Guild navigation.
- **Worm Attraction** – Rhythmic vibrations (harvesters, marching) attract Shai-Hulud. Fremen can ride them.
- **Shield Mechanics** – Shields stop fast projectiles; slow blades penetrate. Lasgun + Shield = catastrophic explosion.
- **Water Management** – Secondary resource, critical for survival. Stillsuits recycle, windtraps collect.
- **Prescience** – Limited fog-of-war reveal + 5-second future prediction for Atreides.
- **The Voice** – Bene Gesserit ability to temporarily control enemy units.

### Playable Factions
1. **House Atreides** – Elite units, ornithopters, water-rich, honorable
2. **House Harkonnen** – Brutal, poison, slave armies, heavy industrial
3. **Fremen** – Desert masters, worm riders, stealth, terraforming goal

- **Mission 5:** Sietch Tabr
- **Mission 6:** Fremen Training
- **Mission 7:** Spice Raids
- **Mission 8:** Alliance with the Fremen
- **Mission 9:** First Worm Ride
- **Mission 10:** Paul Becomes Muad'Dib
- **Mission 11:** Guerilla Warfare
- **Mission 12:** Attack on Arrakeen
- **Mission 13:** Battle Against Sardaukar
- **Mission 14:** Water of Life Ritual
